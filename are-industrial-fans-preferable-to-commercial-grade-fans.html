Are industrial fans preferable to commercial-grade fans?

  • June, 2022
  • Alfa Fans
  • BLOG



It is amazing how your efficiency and return on investment rely on the industrial fan you choose. It goes without saying that you need an industrial fan that is in good operating order, as well as service to support it for many years to come when it comes to such an important component of your business as ventilation equipment.


Because of this, industrial fans should never be used in place of commercial fans. On the other hand, it would be excessive to seek commercial employment as an industrial fan.


But how would a company owner know?

You might be seduced by the low costs certain manufacturers are offering. Remember that they cannot provide the level of quality required to manage demanding projects with a long lifespan.


Cheap commercial or even industrial fans tend to malfunction more quickly, conveniently after the warranty has expired. As a result, you will end up paying significantly more money in the long run.



This tutorial was prepared by Alfa Fans to clarify the distinction between a high-quality fan designed to handle demanding industrial tasks and a commercial fan. Foreign-produced fans typically only cause owners difficulties in the long run.

with a Successful History.

To the untrained eye, the centrifugal fan designs are almost identical. A proper industrial fan, however, needs to be constructed in accordance with an ISO standard. One test that holds industrial fans to a high standard is the examination of the centrifugal fan's speed using finite element analysis.


The following characteristics indicate whether

industrial fans manufactured in India

- Bearings are constructed using the suitable materials (proper casing depending on the bearing material)


- Constructed with heavier gauge materials


- According to the American Bearing Manufacturing Association, shafting with tight tolerance and easily relubricated bearings


- Accepts a high static pressure

What are the best finishes and materials?:-
Before anything else, a supplier has to know where and why you want to use your fan. Then, based on that information, they can advise you on the best material to use for your fan.


Numerous factors are taken into account, including ductwork specifications, temperature restrictions, airstream impurities, etc. Others have reportedly ignored issues like these. One such instance is the use of aluminum fans in places where chlorine gas is present. when the purchaser was not informed that gas corrodes aluminum rapidly.


The finish or final coating is another element that you should be aware of. Some businesses promote "coated steel," which is frequently not what it sounds like. It is merely painted with epoxy or resin. Technically, it is corrosion-resistant, but only if the paint isn't scratched.

Bearings, wheels, and propellers:-

Exhaust fans for factories We suggest that you search for one-piece welded propellers rather than propellers that were created by joining many sections together when it comes to propellers.

Why? since a lot of fans are frail. Pop rivets are therefore required to secure them together. This produces drag, slows down the fan from the start, and shortens its lifespan by putting greater strain on the motor.

The wheels and bearings of your fans are among their most important components. The right materials must be used to make them. For instance, metal bearings don't survive very long with a fiberglass case.

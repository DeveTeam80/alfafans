Industrial Fans for Warehouses: Why You Need Them

  • September, 2021
  • Alfa Fans
  • BLOG



Every warehouse needs industrial fans. Without them food spoils, products dry out, equipment rusts, chemicals and other dangerous vapors sit within the warehouse air, and employees are forced to figure in an uncomfortable and potentially hazardous environment.



Read on to find out more about industrial fans for warehouses and also the best types to include into your distribution center, factory, or warehouse.



Why do warehouses have fans?

Warehouses have fans to make sure quality control and a cushty working environment for workers. Warehouse ventilation fans help regulate humidity and moisture levels, which successively eliminate mold and rust from products and equipment. Industrial fans keep products from drying out or melting. Discover how humidity affects building performance here.



Fans also decrease indoor pollution, keeping dust and debris from your facility. Poor indoor air quality can cause dust to urge into machinery, causing parts to fail or overheat. Indoor air contaminants can even harm employees with health conditions like asthma.



Large industrial warehouse fans provide large amounts of air that produce ventilation and a cushty work environment for employees. When conditions get too hot, employees make mistakes and will suffer from heat related illnesses.



Which fan is common in warehouses?

The two typical varieties of fans you see utilized in warehouses are the centrifugal fan and also the axial fan. Both of those varieties of fans are heavy duty and are usually built to resist hard industrial conditions.



A centrifugal fan blows air out at a 90 degree angle and spins the air by force and deflection. Centrifugal fans tend to be loud and increase the speed of air through impellers. A standard centrifugal fan is known as the blower.



An axial fan has blades that rotate around an axis. Air is drawn in parallel to the axis then blown into a similar direction. An example of an axial fan would be the industrial ceiling fan, also called a high volume low speed fan or HVLS fan.



Why are industrial fans stronger than standard fans?

Industrial fans can move more air than standard fans can. they're stronger than traditional fans because factories and warehouses need more power in a fan because of the amount of space, product, or chemicals present within the building.



A standard fan is typically purchased to cool down one room during a home for one or two occupants, while one industrial fan can be used to cool down 10 people in a wide open space. determine the most effective industrial shop fan here.



What are industrial ceiling fans?

A commercial ceiling fan is larger than a standard residential ceiling fan. Warehouse ceiling fans, or Industrial wall fans, rely on their large blade sizes and low speeds to effectively improve air flow, air circulation and ventilation in a warehouse. Unlike residential ceiling fans, which are loud and choppy, Industrial wall fans move quietly and smoothly.



An Industrial wall industrial ceiling fan is the ideal fan for any industrial space. An Industrial wall fan is inexpensive to control and leads to safer working conditions, increased productivity, and decreased energy costs regardless of the season, among other benefits.

Where are industrial ceiling fans used?

Industrial ceiling fans are used in warehouses and factories to maintain a cushty and safe temperature for workers and machinery. Warehouse fans provide climate control and keep unwanted condensation off of equipment, products, and supplies.



Industrial wall fans are perfect for agricultural, commercial, and industrial spaces thanks to their size. Industrial wall fans are greater than seven feet in diameter and are ideal for wide open spaces and buildings with high ceilings.



Industries that use Industrial wall fans include airports, automotive, barns, distribution centers, manufacturing plants, and warehouses.

Air movement, air quality, and warehouse ventilation are critical for the health of your products, facility, and employees. If your distribution center or factory is outfitted with the suitable industrial fans for the kind of labor and products you produce you'll have a successful operation and happy, productive employees.

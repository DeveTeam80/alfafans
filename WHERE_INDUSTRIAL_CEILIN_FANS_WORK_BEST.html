WHERE INDUSTRIAL CEILING FANS WORK BEST

  • January, 2022
  • Alfa Fans
  • BLOG



Industrial ceiling fans are selected and installed as per the specific requirements of the end-user location. However, it does not matter where your industrial facility is located, you will have to deal with the local climatic conditions. The temperature and humidity will vary based on your geographical location, just as it will with the seasons.



For this very reason your industrial heating and cooling system will be working all year long. It works on the principle of an HVAC system. Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) is the use of different technologies to control the temperature, humidity, and purity of the air in an enclosed space. Its major goal is to provide thermal comfort and acceptable indoor air quality. While designing an air-conditioning system, be it for cooling or heating the space, operating costs have to be taken under consideration. Without a proper setup plan, the cost for heating and cooling your industrial building can be quite expensive. If you own an industrial facility of any kind, an industrial ceiling fan is an essential machine to own.



A proper fan helps maintain an even coolness in the summertime without costing you any additional air conditioning costs, the right kind of industrial ceiling fan can also maintain a constant level of warmth in the winters, and also provide the same cost savings by limiting the amount of heat involvement essential in the winter seasons.


According to industry experts, in many cases the best type of answer lies with a large industrial fan, which stands for HIGH-VOLUME, LOW SPEED.


What Is A Large Industrial Fan?


A large industrial fan is a type of fan with blades of seven feet or more. Large industrial fans are commonly used in the commercial, refrigeration and agricultural industries. The major reason for using these structures is due to large, high-ceilinged open-concept buildings.

The main purpose of an industrial fan is to direct a large flow of air or gas to various processes. This is usually provided through a rotation of blades which are connected to a hub/shaft, and driven by a motor. There are many uses for the continuous flow of air or gas that industrial fans generate including: combustion, ventilation, exhaust, and particulate transport, heating and cooling are few of them. These fans operate in both clockwise and anti-clockwise orientations, and common accessories for fans include: flanges, shaft seals, belts, and bearing guards. Belt/shafts and bearing guards are extremely important as they form the major supporting structure of the fan system.

Industrial ceiling fans keep fans much above the ground to conserve your precious floor space thereby eliminating the hazard of tripping or running into fans on the floors. Large industrial fans have a powerful electric motor that generates massive amounts of airflow by using the high volume, low-speed method, that means they have large blades that move a high volumetric area, but at a relatively slower speed than other fan types. Industrial ceiling fans work by creating air movement from ceiling to floor, which increases circulation, helps to control humidity, and conserves warm air and heat in colder months. In addition, they are developed to have a very low energy consumption rate during operations.

Industrial Fans Operating And Electricity Cost


Considering cost and energy use, doubling the speed of fan blades costs four times as much energy, and tripling the speed of fan blades costs eight times as much energy. The relationship of a fan speed relative to cost is not linear. In fact, quadrupling fan speed will cost 16 times as much energy. Hence, the slowly-moving blades of a large industrial plant are such an important cost-savings decision. Industrial ceiling fans are capable of creating the necessary air movement and associated air circulation that can be a benefit to indoor industrial environments and buildings, but at a much lower cost.

Due to this very reason and minimum operational speed it consumes less electricity while functioning.

  • Manufacturing Plants
  • Barns
  • Distribution Centers
  • Auto industry
  • Shopping Malls
  • Airports
  • Sports Centers

  • Cannabis Production Facilities
  • Dairy Farms

    • HOW YOU WILL BENEFIT FROM AN INDUSTRIAL CEILING FAN

    In case you own and operate a building in the commercial, industrial, or agricultural sectors, you probably are facing some pain points that an industrial fan can solve. Large open-concept buildings are vulnerable to moisture and airborne contaminants, which can include volatile organic compounds, pollen, and chemical fumes. Without proper air circulation, this can lead to Sick Building Syndrome (SBS).

    Working staff, guest visitors, and even farm animals can experience negative health effects, which can range from headaches to nausea and dizziness to respiratory diseases. An industrial large industrial fan promotes better air circulation, so your staff, visitors, and animals can breathe easily while inside the building. Installing industrial fans in your facility will help work productivity to remain high as the fans will regulate the temperature. Large facilities often have trapped air pockets and uneven temperatures. Which will lead your HVAC system to work overtime, an expensive proposition in the hottest summer temperatures or the coldest winter weathers. Industrial fans are cost-effective solutions in this respect, and they keep the hot and cold air circulating so that your facility will be operating at an ideal temperature.

    MAINTAIN YOUR FACILITY’S PRODUCTIVITY

    Not all air-conditioning solutions need to be complicated or expensive. A simple, expertly designed industrial fan solves many problems facilities face nowadays and can actually save you money. For optimal heating, cooling, ventilation and longer-lasting stored products or equipment, a large industrial fan is often the best answer.

Ventilation With Industrial Fans To Fight Heat Stress

  • March, 2022
  • Alfa Fans
  • BLOG



When summer temperatures rise, heat stress, heatstroke, and heat stroke are all concerns. Industrial fans and

ventilation systems

help keep workers cool and reduce the risk of health and safety hazards from extreme heat. A hot environment can cause overheating, which can lead to heat-related illnesses and even heat-related deaths. Overheating affects productivity and can lead to other injuries. Airflow and ventilation strategies can support the body's natural cooling system.


Industrial Fans and Ventilation Systems Plays a Key Role in Maintaining Worker Productivity

As you may know, humans maintain a stable internal temperature of about 98.6° F. There is also a sophisticated natural system that helps to keep you warm when it's cold and cool off when it's hot. The main way we cool down is by sweating. As the sweat evaporates from the skin, it can remove some of the excess heat and cool it. However, in very hot and humid environments such as industrial facilities, cooling this way can be more difficult. Sweat may not evaporate effectively or quickly, which can lead to overheating and heat stress in the worker. Industrial fans for summer and ventilation systems can support this process to reduce the risk of overheating. The ventilation system can reduce the risk of heat stress. These systems help the fan dissipate excess heat inside while maintaining air circulation. The fan does not lower the temperature, but it helps to increase airflow and circulation and speed up the sweat evaporation process. It feels refreshing and comfortable, like the breeze on a hot day.

The Dangers of Heat Stress & Heat Stroke

Overheating has many severe dangers which unfortunately people are unaware of. For the fact, several people suffer problems or even die each year from heat-related illnesses.

Below are given common dangers of overheating which includes:

  • Heat Cramps
  • Heat Rash
  • Fainting
  • Heat Exhaustion
  • Heat Stroke

    • Usually, When the body loses excess water and salt, heat exhaustion occurs. Heat exhaustion is eventually a result of profuse sweating. It can cause confusion, dizziness, fainting, rapid heartbeat, and other serious symptoms. If this problem is not treated quickly, this can turn into heatstroke, which is a potentially life-threatening illness that can cause death or permanent disability. Also, extremely hot environments can also lead to other safety hazards. For example, impaired judgment, lack of mental clarity, cloudy goggles, and hard-to-grip sweaty hands.

    Therefore, it is important to reduce the risk of heatstroke for employees. Some common solutions to this are encouraging frequent breaks, encouraging workers to drink large amounts of water, and installing industrial ventilation control systems that help lower room temperatures.

    Industrial Ventilation System Control Alternatives

    There are many options for improving ventilation, airflow, and temperature in industrial facilities. It is important to consider potential risks when designing a ventilation system that works effectively in a facility. For example, a facility at risk of high temperature and humidity may require a different ventilation solution than a facility that may only be exposed to high temperatures. If humidity is also an issue, ventilation experts may recommend a dehumidifier. For facilities that require lower temperatures but live in mostly dry climates, the evaporative cooling unit helps to lower the temperature without adding excess moisture to the air. Of course,

    high-quality industrial fans

    are suitable for increasing airflow in most facilities. The ventilation system has so many options that it is imperative to work with an experienced ventilation specialist at the facility.
